I thought I would shoot you the answers to a few common questions I often get about Timez Attack and Multiplication Mastery:

  1. How long does it usually take a child to finish?

    We've tested pre-3rd graders for an hour a day for 9 days.  50% of them passed off all the levels, 33% were on the 9's, and 16% were struggling down in the 4's.

  2. What can I do to help my child progress even faster?

    Ok, it's a shameless plug, but if you can afford the full version, the additional environments will really help a lot.  In addition to that, taking time to pin down some concrete goals really does work wonders.  If all else fails, there's always good old-fashioned bribery.

  3. Do children need anything besides Timez Attack?

    Definitely.  Timez Attack is exceptionally good at teaching children total mastery of the multiplication tables, but it's still vital that children learn how to apply and manipulate all those facts.  Be sure they get ample opportunity to move all that monitor/keyboard knowledge over to pencil/paper answers.
