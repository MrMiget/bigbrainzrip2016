2010 - 2011 School Year

 

Results

The blue bars on the left represent the pre-test scores of 250,000 students during the 2010 - 2011 schoool year. Specifically, this means they were asked each each of the multiplication facts, 2 - 12 and their response was timed and evaluated, factoring in the time required for typing.

 

For this analysis the child was allowed a generous response time of 2 seconds, with additional time to type the answer. In other words, this is a generous assessment of mastery. Most of the students are from 3rd and 4th grade. The bar on the right shows the fluency scores of the 15,000 students who played through Timez Attack. Average playing time for that group was 7 to 8 hours.

 

 


Based on 15,000 Pre-Test and Post-Test Scores from 500 Districts Nationwide

Study Details

Each student is given a pre-test and a post-test, where they are asked all 66 facts, 2 - 12, with randomized reciprocals.
Timez Attack records whether each fact was answered correctly within the allocated time.
The results suggest that nearly every student, regardless of income, race, grade, or geography
finishes with the ability to instantly recall every single fact perfectly, with only 1 or 2 mistakes.
